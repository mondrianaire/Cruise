v.135: tight 214x1449 canvas — kill the cream margin around the ship

Reported problem: deck-plan container appeared too wide and dashed
venue hotspot borders extended into the cream margin outside the
ship outline.

Two contributing factors fixed:

1) PNG margin. v.133/v.134 cropped to 230x1449 with 4-6 px of
   padding either side of the ship outline. Tightened the crop:
   union of all decks' horizontal content extents (x=24..238) with
   only 1 px breathing room. New canonical canvas: 214x1449.

2) Container padding leak. An older .fp-img-stage rule still set
   padding:8px 6px 6px !important, which made the container's
   inner content box wider than the image with cream visible on
   both sides. Force padding:0 in v.135, and replace the
   aspect-ratio:230/1449 lock with an explicit
   width: calc(88vh * 214 / 1449)
   so the container is guaranteed to match the new canvas exactly,
   even in browsers where aspect-ratio is finicky.

Per-deck horizontal extents now confirmed within 24..238 every deck:
  deck-01: 25-237   deck-02: 38-224   deck-03: 38-224
  deck-04: 25-237   deck-05: 27-233   deck-06: 29-232
  deck-07: 28-233   deck-08: 33-228   deck-09: 34-228
  deck-10: 36-225   deck-11: 33-222

So at most 9 px of internal white margin on any deck. Hotspots
positioned with left:18% width:64% now stay entirely inside the
ship hull.
