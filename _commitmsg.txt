v.117: landscape deck-pick prompt is now screen-relative + full-screen

When the user picks a deck from the cross-section while in landscape on
mobile/tablet, the rotate-to-portrait prompt was a small bottom-of-page
toast. It moved with scroll, was easy to miss, and pinch-zoom could
push it off-screen.

Rebuilt as a full-screen overlay (same .rotate-overlay structure as
the v.113 initial portrait gate):
  - position:fixed, inset:0  - screen-relative, scroll-independent,
    pinch-zoom safe.
  - Big centered card on dark navy backdrop.
  - Animated reverse-rotating phone glyph (this one rotates landscape ->
    portrait, the opposite direction of the initial gate).
  - Title: "To view Deck X"
  - Message: "Rotate your device to portrait mode. We'll load
    [Deck Name] the moment you turn it."
  - Big red [ CANCEL ] button clears pendingDeckJump and dismisses,
    leaving the user on the cross-section view they were on.
  - body.style.overflow=hidden while open (no underlying scroll).
  - No auto-dismiss timer - the user must rotate OR cancel.

When the device rotates to portrait, autoOrientView dismisses the
overlay, restores body scroll, then applies the pending deck on the
deck-plans view.

Removed the legacy v.103 toast CSS block (the structure is now shared
with the orientation gate).
