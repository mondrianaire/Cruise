# LESSONS — Canada & New England 2026 postmortem

These are mistakes the planner made on the *real* trip, captured the day
they were discovered so the next trip hub doesn't repeat them.

## Major miss: 2026 FIFA World Cup overlap

**What happened.** The trip ran Jun 11–21, 2026. The 2026 FIFA World Cup
opened Jun 11, 2026 — the same day as the group's arrival into Montreal.
Gillette Stadium (Boston / Foxborough) was one of the 11 US host cities.
Toronto and Vancouver were the Canadian hosts. The trip therefore sat
inside the first 10 days of the tournament, with Boston as a host city
and Montreal in a host country.

**What the planner did.** Nothing. There was no calendar overlay of
major scheduled global events against the trip window. The hub
surfaced nothing about the World Cup on any of the affected days.

**Why it mattered — the real harm.** The miss wasn't atmospheric, it
was decision-grade. The travellers reported, after the trip, that
being in town during a **USA** match (Boston) or a **CAN** match
(Montreal) was enough to have driven itinerary changes — different
hotel night, different drive plan, different dinner reservation
strategy, possibly different port-day choices. Pre-trip intel that
doesn't change the plan is FYI; pre-trip intel that *would* have
changed the plan, withheld, is a failure of the hub's purpose.

**The pattern to avoid.** Treating "is there a famous event" as the
question. The right question is **"would I plan around it if I knew."**
Atmosphere callouts go in the day notes; decision-grade overlaps get
flagged on the affected day with a clear recommendation: **avoid,
embrace, or shift.**

**The fix.** See [`tools/pre-trip-checklist.md`](tools/pre-trip-checklist.md).
A global-events crosscheck is now a required step before any future
trip hub is considered ready.
