Activity levels explained

EasyMostly seated touring; little walking, on even ground.
ModerateSustained walking; some stairs or uneven paths.
StrenuousLong distances on foot, steep grades, or rough terrain.

These are Holland America's official ratings and describe physical demand — not how long a tour is, or how interesting it is. They can read oddly against the clock: a few short walking tours rate Strenuous because of sustained walking on cobbled or uneven streets, while some seven-hour coach tours rate only Moderate because guests are seated for most of the day. Always read the level together with the listed duration when judging a tour, and confirm in the Navigator app before booking.

Day 1 • Jun 13 • Embarkation

Quebec City, Quebec

The only walled city north of Mexico and a UNESCO World Heritage Site — Old Quebec's cobbled lanes climb from Quartier Petit-Champlain to Château Frontenac. Most of the day is for boarding.

Day 4 • Jun 16 • 7 AM–5 PM

Charlottetown, PEI

Birthplace of Confederation and Anne of Green Gables country — red-sand shores, lighthouses, lobster, and the 12.9-km Confederation Bridge.

Day 5 • Jun 17 • 7 AM–4 PM

Sydney, Nova Scotia

The heart of Cape Breton — Highlands scenery, the Cabot Trail, the Fortress of Louisbourg, and live Celtic fiddle music everywhere.

Day 6 • Jun 18 • 8 AM–4 PM

Halifax, Nova Scotia

A lively harbour capital — the star-shaped Citadel, the Maritime Museum's Titanic collection, and iconic Peggy's Cove nearby. Pinnacle Grill dinner is 6 PM tonight.

Day 7 • Jun 19 • 12:30–10 PM

Portland, Maine

New England's foodie town on Casco Bay — the Old Port's lobster shacks and breweries, Portland Head Light, and a long evening in port.

Day 8 • Jun 20 • Disembarkation

Boston, Massachusetts

The voyage ends in historic Boston — the 2.5-mile Freedom Trail, Faneuil Hall and the North End, with a hotel night before the June 21 flight.
